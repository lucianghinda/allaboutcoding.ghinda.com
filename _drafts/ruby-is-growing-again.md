I see indications that Ruby is growing, or at least, I observe clear signs that people using Ruby have increased confidence in its growth.

I don't have some exact numbers for the number of people learning Ruby and the number of companies/products that started with their tech stack being Ruby based. For both the results of this growth will be seen after maybe after one year.

But what I saw in 2022 is new courses, new podcasts, new conferences, and new resources launched.

This trend seems to be taking a quicker pace in the first half of 2023

---

## New conferences

There are new Ruby conferences freshly launched. Here are some examples:

* [Friendly.rb](https://friendlyrb.com)
    
* [Sin City Ruby](https://www.sincityruby.com)
    
* [Ruby Conf Thailand](https://rubyconfth.com)
    
* [RailsSaaS](https://railssaas.com)
    
* [Helevtic Ruby](https://helvetic-ruby.ch)
    
* [RubyConf Mini](https://www.rubyconfmini.com)
    
* [Blue Ridge Ruby](https://blueridgeruby.com)
    
* [Helvetic Ruby](https://helvetic-ruby.ch)
    
* [Ruby Unconf EU](https://2023.rubyunconf.eu)
    

Some older events may be stopped due to Covid or some other causes in the previous years are now resumed:

* [Rocky Mountain Ruby](https://rockymtnruby.dev)
    
* [South East Ruby](http://southeastruby.com)
    

This is not a comprehensive list of conferences. Visit [https://rubyconferences.org](https://rubyconferences.org) for an up-to-date list.

But I wanted to show that there are new conferences organised.

This is a good sign meaning that there is an appetite for more Ruby content and also that the market of people or companies willing to pay to participate is growing
